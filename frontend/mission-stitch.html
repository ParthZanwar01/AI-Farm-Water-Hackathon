AquaCool Mission Overview

Observe

Continuous thermal telemetry across 24 server zones in four racks at a one-second cadence — temperature, cooling state, and neighbor diffusion feed a rolling buffer for training and live inference.

Infer

Ensemble ML (gradient boosting / random forest) scores spike probability from 37+ engineered features — lags, rolling stats, rack neighbors, calendars — pre-authorizing cooling above ~70% confidence before hard thresholds.

Interdict

Targeted partial flow (~0.8 gal per proactive event) replaces reactive deluge (~2.0 gal). A failsafe still trips at 85°F — but in AI mode the goal is to never need it.

The Problem We Solve

AI clusters push data-center heat loads faster than many sites can justify fresh water for evaporative towers. Default controls wait for danger, then run cooling at maximum — wasting water and stressing hardware.

Reactive systems only act after temperatures breach a trip line (e.g. 85°F). By then the plant needs full flow. There is no anticipation — only reaction — so every event pays the highest hydraulic and thermal price.

AquaCool closes the loop: predict the spike from live patterns, cool earlier in a narrower band, and shut off aggressively — delivering the same safety class with materially lower water per intervention.
