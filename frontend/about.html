The Problem

Data centers are running out of water

As AI workloads explode, so does data center heat — and the water used to cool it. Traditional reactive cooling is the default, but it's also the most wasteful.

200B

Gallons per year

Global data centers consume an estimated 200 billion gallons of water annually for cooling — equivalent to the water needs of millions of households. That number grows every year with AI demand.

3–5M

Gallons per day

A single large hyperscale data center can consume 3 to 5 million gallons per day via evaporative cooling towers — more than a small city's daily water usage.

0

Seconds of advance warning

Standard reactive systems only trigger cooling after a server exceeds the 85°F threshold. There's no prediction, no anticipation — just reaction. By then, full cooling capacity is needed.

How It Works

Three stages of intelligent cooling

AquaCool AI combines real-time physics simulation, machine learning inference, and adaptive control to stay ahead of heat — not behind it.

01

Monitor

All 24 servers are sampled every second. Temperature, cooling state, and rack topology are continuously fed into a rolling data buffer.

1-second tick rate across all 24 nodes
Heat diffusion modeled between rack neighbors
Spatial topology: 4 racks × 6 servers, up/down/left/right neighbors
All readings persisted to training dataset
02

Predict

A Gradient Boosting classifier analyzes 37+ features per server per tick, outputting a spike probability. Above 70% → cooling is pre-authorized.

Temporal: hour, day, business hours, cyclical encoding
Lag: last 1, 2, 3, 5, and 10 readings per server
Rolling stats: 3/6/12/24-step mean, std, max, min
Spatial: neighbor mean/max/std and spike count
Trend: 1-step and 3-step acceleration
03

Act

Proactive cooling activates before the threshold is hit. The system uses just enough water to intercept the spike — then shuts off automatically.

Proactive trigger: spike_probability > 0.70
Failsafe trigger: temp > 85°F (reactive backup)
Proactive = 0.8 gal/event vs reactive 2.0 gal/event
Auto-off at 68°F — no over-cooling waste
